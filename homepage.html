Understanding the “classifications” for cannabis strains: Indica and Sativa have been the 2 main ways to classify marijuania for as long as the drug has been studied. Indica is said to have a more relaxing and sedative effect on the body with earthier aromas. Whereas Sativa is more of a stimulant meant to boost energy and has a sweeter aroma(a fun way to remember the difference is when thinking indica, think indacouch). However, in the current world stain types are ALL more hybrid than they are indica or sativa. There are consistent new studies that keep drawing the same conclusion when attempting to compare the 2 strain types. Sophie Watts a PHD student in Plant, Food and Environmental Sciences at Dalhousie University has written a paper on the improper comparison of indica and sativa stains. They compared the genetics of different indica and sativa stains where they found inconsistencies throughout the history of the labeling of stains that she called the “telephone” effect. They concluded that current marijuania strains consistently have strong genetic ties to both indica and sativa stains ruling them ALL as hybrids. They even found cases where a stain was originally classed as indica and then was later classed as a sativa. “An example that illustrates the inconsistent use of these labels is that in 1999, a cannabis strain named “AK 47” won the Sativa Cup in the Cannabis Cup. The same strain went on to win the Indica Cup in the same competition four years later.” While genetically there is almost no difference in these strains, the effects of each stain can be very different, so it is important to know that indica sativa and hybrid are more of an “effect” category and less of a genetic identifier. In general marijuania with a higher cbd % will tend to have a more mellowing effect. While the genetic variation within the many strains of cannabis has become less unique there are still vastly different in terms of the medicinal effects it has, in general it can be understood that marujania with higher CBD% tend to have a more “mellowing” effect that's often associated with the indica classification. What is Cannabis?

Cannabis is a type of flower plant also known as marijuania. There are hundreds of different strains that the drug comes in and new genetic variants are constantly being made. All of which are classified into 3 main categories; indica, sativa, hybrids. The intensity of the drug heavily depends on the amount of tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) or cannabidiol (CBD) they have due to the psychoactive properties within the cannabinoids. The distinct flavor/aroma of each strain is dependent on the terpenes. So when smoking something with a distinct taste and smell it can be said that it's a very terpy strain.
